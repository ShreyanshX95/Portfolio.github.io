My research focuses on developing novel deep learning architectures that are both computationally efficient and highly accurate, pushing the boundaries of what's possible in medical and historical data analysis.

PUBLISHED · IEEE IC-CGU 2025

Early Brain Tumor Detection Using Dynamic Spectrum & Modified Swin Transformer

This published work introduces a training-free model that achieves 97.25% accuracy. It uses a novel Dynamic Spectrum method for localizing tumor-prone regions and an Expanding Dynamic Window logic to refine detection, resulting in a state-of-the-art inference time of just 0.16s.

AuraVision: Swinir, FFC and Frequency Analysis to Restore Manuscript and Old Data

My current research, "AuraVision," investigates the use of SWinir models and Fast Fourier Convolutions (FFC) to restore heavily degraded historical manuscripts. By analyzing frequency domain data, this model aims to reconstruct unreadable text and images.
